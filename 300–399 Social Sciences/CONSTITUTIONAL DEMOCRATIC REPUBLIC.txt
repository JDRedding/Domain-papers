CONSTITUTIONAL DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC 

A tri‑mode governance manifold with orthogonal operators:
C‑mode (Constraint), L‑mode (Legitimacy), M‑mode (Mechanism)

C‑MODE : CONSTITUTIONAL CONSTRAINT  (SID‑Structure)
------------------------------------------------------------
S : Enumerated Rights
I : Limited Institutions
D : Procedural Safeguards

C‑mode defines the allowable state‑space and boundary conditions.
It prevents runaway majority dynamics by fixing the manifold geometry.

RDG Mapping:
  SID.Structure   = Rights as S‑tokens
  SID.Interaction = Institutional limits as I‑tokens
  SID.Dynamics    = Procedural safeguards as D‑tokens

Formal:
  C = SID(S, I, D)

L‑MODE : DEMOCRATIC LEGITIMACY  (PED‑Evaluation)
------------------------------------------------------------
p : Population Input
P : Evaluation Signal (popular sovereignty)
E : Legitimacy Operator

L‑mode supplies the evaluation signal grounding authority in the
population manifold. Authority originates from the people.

RDG Mapping:
  PED.Power      = p → P (popular input)
  PED.Evaluation = legitimacy operator E
  PED.Dynamics   = update of authority signals

Formal:
  L = PED(p → P, E)

M‑MODE : REPUBLICAN MECHANISM  (SID‑Interaction + Q‑Moderation)
------------------------------------------------------------
R : Representative Channels
Lw: Law as Update Rule
Q□: Moderated Transition Operator

M‑mode is the moderated transition operator acting through
representative channels. Governance is mediated, not direct.

RDG Mapping:
  SID.Interaction = Representatives R
  SID.Dynamics    = Law Lw
  Q‑Moderation    = Q□ (institutional moderation of transitions)

Formal:
  M = SID(R, Lw) ⊗ Q□

SYNTHESIS
------------------------------------------------------------
Governance Manifold G:

  G = C ⊗ L ⊗ M

Expanded:

  G = SID(S, I, D)
      ⊗ PED(p → P, E)
      ⊗ [ SID(R, Lw) ⊗ Q□ ]

Interpretation:
  - C‑mode fixes the geometry (constraints)
  - L‑mode supplies the evaluation signal (legitimacy)
  - M‑mode executes moderated transitions (mechanism)

SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------
A constitutional democratic republic is:
  C‑mode in constraint,
  L‑mode in legitimacy,
  M‑mode in mechanism
a tri‑mode governance manifold correcting the instability
of pure democracy by structuring authority, moderating transitions,
and grounding evaluation in the population.
