JOT‑TERMS - Justified Object Token (TERMS Layer)
============================================================
JOT‑TERMS is defined entirely outside the  RDG(SID/PED) / RDGMFEQ kernel.  
Below is the formal specification, modular and non‑intrusive.

JOT‑TERMS is the governed representational unit built on TERMS.
It binds:
    • a Thing‑token (⧈)
    • a Representation‑token (⧉)
    • a Constitutional Signature (Σ)
    • a Justification Statement (Admissibility)

TERMS foundation
JOT‑TERMS sits directly on TERMS, which provides:

⧈ — thing‑token
⧉ — representation‑token
↦, ↤, ↮ — mapping algebra
domain separation (𝒯 ∩ ℛ = ∅)
recoverability
non‑collapse rules

JOT‑TERMS does not modify TERMS — it extends it with governance metadata.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. TERMS DOMAINS
------------------------------------------------------------
Justified Object Token (TERMS‑layer governed representational unit)

A JOT‑TERMS is the governed representational unit constructed on top of the TERMS substrate.
It binds a thing‑token, a representation‑token, and a governance signature into a single explicit object.

A JOT‑TERMS binds three things:

	A thing‑token (⧈)
	A representation‑token (⧉)
	A constitutional signature (Σ)

Thing‑token domain:
    ⧈ ∈ 𝒯

Representation‑token domain:
    ⧉ ∈ ℛ

Domain separation invariant:
    𝒯 ∩ ℛ = ∅
       ⧈ ≠ ⧉


Justified Object Token contains the justification statement (representational admissibility)

This creates a governed representational unit that is explicit, recoverable, and structurally stable.

    ⧈  ↮  ⧉

4. JUSTIFICATION LAYER
------------------------------------------------------------
Justification = Admissibility(⧈ ↮ ⧉)

This is NOT RDGMFEQ admissibility.
It is representational admissibility:
    • What makes the mapping valid?
    • What commitments it relies on?
    • What constraints it obeys?
    • What assumptions are declared?

6. INVARIANTS
------------------------------------------------------------
Explicitness:
    All components must be explicit.

Recoverability:
    JOT‑TERMS must be reconstructible from Σ.

Identity Preservation:
    CID must remain stable across revisions.

Domain Separation:
    ⧈ and ⧉ remain distinct.

Non‑collapse:
    No identity morphisms; no conflation of thing and representation.

Signature Completeness:
    Σ must contain all required fields.
