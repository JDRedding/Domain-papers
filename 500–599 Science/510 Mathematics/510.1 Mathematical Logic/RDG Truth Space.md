### Connectives ↔ RDG operators

Take four core TML connectives:

- **¬ (negation)**  
- **∧ (conjunction)**  
- **∨ (disjunction)**  
- **→ (implication)**  

RDG/MFE operator geometry:

- **¬x = 1 − x**
  
  ↔ **SID↔PED inversion operator**: flip dominance along the SID/PED axis while keeping flux magnitude.  

  Geometrically: reflect a state across the triadic center—swap “which side” of the coupling is dominant.

- **x ∧ y = min(x,y)**
  
  ↔ **intersection of constraints / bottleneck operator**: the joint system is only as strong as the weakest SID/PED support.  

  In RDG: overlapping relational constraints; in MFE: shared limiting flux channel.

- **x ∨ y = max(x,y)**
  
  ↔ **union of channels / enabling operator**: system can ride whichever SID/PED path has higher support.  

  In RDG: alternative relational routes; in MFE: multiple flux paths, strongest wins.

- **x → y (graded, contextual)**
  
  ↔ **directed coupling operator**: “how well does state x drive state y” along SID/PED geometry.  

  If $$\(x \le y\)$$: full support (1) → SID/PED alignment.  
  If $$\(x > y\)$$: penalty → mismatch between source and target geometry.

TML connectives are **direct encodings of RDG/MFE operators on the SID/PED axis**.

### Sequent calculus ↔ RDG/MFE process chains

Triadic sequent:

- $$\(\Gamma \vdash_{\tau} \Delta\)$$

RDG/MFE:

- **Γ** = initial configuration of SID/PED operators (geometry + flux setup)  
- **Δ** = resulting configuration (target geometry/flux)  
- **τ** = quality of coupling (how well the process chain actually realizes Δ from Γ)

A high‑τ sequent is a **good RDG/MFE process chain**; low‑τ is a leaky, misaligned, or frustrated chain.
