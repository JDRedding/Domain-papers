              OPERATOR NAMING GRAMMAR
                 (Redding Notation)
============================================================

This document defines a general naming scheme for operators
of the form:

    (generator)_(type)^(regime)

This serves as a universal annotation style for generative,
transformative, or evaluative operators across all systems.

The canonical operator signature is:

    (generator)_(type)^(regime)

where:

    Generator : the operator family
    Type      : the functional role
    Regime    : the operational mode

More formally:

    Operator = G_t^r

with:

    G ∈ Generator Family
    t ∈ Operator Types
    r ∈ Operational Regimes

This makes every operator a member of a three-level taxonomy.


------------------------------------------------------------
1. CORE PATTERN
------------------------------------------------------------

Every operator follows the schema:

    (generator)_(noun/type)^(verb/adjective)

Where:

    generator      = the base operator (Q, Xi, T, D, etc.)
    noun/type      = operator category or functional role
    verb/adjective = regime, mode, or rule in effect

This pattern is independent of any specific theory.


------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 1 — GENERATOR (Base Family)
------------------------------------------------------------

Answers the question:

    "What family does this belong to?"

Examples:

    Q     		Q	admissibility
    Xi    		Ξ	transformation
    Gamma 		Γ	closure
    Delta 		Δ	difference
    Sigma 		Σ	aggregation
    Pi    		Π	projection
    Lambda 		Λ	lifting
    T     		T	transport
    D     		D	dynamics

These are families, not specific operators.

------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 2 — TYPE (Subscript)
------------------------------------------------------------

Answers the question:

    "What does this operator do?"

Examples:

    a   admissibility
    t   transformation
    c   closure
    p   projection
    e   evaluation
    n   normalization
    f   filtering
    m   measurement
    g   generation

These are roles, not implementations.


------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 3 — REGIME (Superscript)
------------------------------------------------------------

Answers the question:

    "Under what rules is it operating?"

Numeric regimes:

    1   classical
    2   damped
    3   hysteretic
    4   stochastic
    5   quantum
    6   nonlinear

Symbolic regimes:

    S   structural
    F   field
    D   dynamic
    L   local
    G   global
    A   adaptive

The superscript is a policy selector.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. LONG-FORM vs SHORT-FORM
------------------------------------------------------------

Long-form (first appearance):

    Q_admissibility^classical
    Xi_transformation^structural

Short-form (after definition):

    Q_a^1
    Xi_t^S

Mapping:

    Q_admissibility^classical  -->  Q_a^1


------------------------------------------------------------
3. EXAMPLE: ADMISSIBILITY GENERATORS
------------------------------------------------------------

Long-form definitions:

    Q_admissibility^classical   = i
    Q_admissibility^damped      = i + delta_damped
    Q_admissibility^hysteretic  = i + delta_hysteretic

Short-form equivalents:

    Q_a^1 = i
    Q_a^2 = i + delta_a^2
    Q_a^3 = i + delta_a^3


------------------------------------------------------------
4. EXAMPLE: XI-OPERATORS
------------------------------------------------------------

Long-form:

    Xi_transformation^structural
    Xi_transformation^field
    Xi_transformation^dynamic

Short-form:

    Xi_t^S
    Xi_t^F
    Xi_t^D


------------------------------------------------------------
5. SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------

All operators follow:

    (generator)_(type)^(regime)

Rules:

    - Long-form is used on first introduction.
    - Short-form is used thereafter.
    - Superscripts index regimes.
    - Subscripts denote operator type.

Canonical example:

    Q_admissibility^classical = Q_a^1 = i

------------------------------------------------------------
6. DOCUMENT-LOCAL ALIASES
------------------------------------------------------------

The canonical operator identifier is always given by

    G_t^r

or its long-form equivalent.

After an operator has been introduced, authors may define
document-local aliases or abbreviations to improve readability.

Examples:

    Q_a^1  := Q₀
    Γ_c^A  := Γ*
    Ξ_t^S  := ΞS

Such aliases are purely typographical conveniences and have
no semantic status outside the document in which they are
defined.

All aliases should be listed in an appendix or notation table.

The canonical identifier G_t^r remains the authoritative
reference.

============================================================
CANONICAL FORM

    G_t^r

Examples:

    Q_a^1     classical admissibility generator
    Q_a^3     hysteretic admissibility generator
    Xi_t^S    structural transformation operator
    Pi_p^L    local projection operator
    D_m^4     stochastic measurement operator
